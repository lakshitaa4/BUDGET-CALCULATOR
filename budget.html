Tax Deductions Available Under the 2025 Budget

The Indian Budget for 2025 introduces a variety of tax deductions that can help you reduce your taxable income. Here's a breakdown of the available deductions:

Section 80C: Investments and Savings

Section 80C allows you to claim deductions for investments made in specific instruments. The maximum amount you can claim under this section is ₹1,50,000. Some of the eligible instruments include:

  • Pension funds
  • Public Provident Fund (PPF)
  • Life insurance premiums
  • Employee Provident Fund (EPF)
  • National Savings Certificate (NSC)
  • Five-year fixed deposit with banks

Section 80D: Health Insurance Premium

You can claim deductions under Section 80D for premiums paid on health insurance policies for yourself, your family, and parents. The maximum deduction available is:

  • ₹25,000 for self and family (₹50,000 if senior citizens)
  • ₹50,000 for parents (₹1,00,000 if senior citizens)

Section 24(b): Home Loan Interest

If you have a home loan, you can claim a deduction of up to ₹2,00,000 on the interest paid on your home loan under Section 24(b) of the Income Tax Act. This deduction applies to both self-occupied and rented properties.

Section 10(14): House Rent Allowance (HRA)

If you receive HRA from your employer and live in a rented house, you may be eligible for a deduction under Section 10(14). The deduction amount is calculated based on factors such as the salary, rent paid, and the city you live in. Ensure you provide accurate details when claiming this deduction.

Section 80E: Education Loan

Under Section 80E, you can claim deductions on the interest paid on loans taken for higher education. There is no upper limit for this deduction, and it applies to loans taken for self, spouse, children, or for a student for whom the taxpayer is a legal guardian.

Section 80G: Donations to Charitable Institutions

Donations made to recognized charitable institutions can be claimed as deductions under Section 80G. You can claim a deduction of up to 50% or 100% of the donated amount, depending on the type of institution and the specific conditions.

Note: The deductions mentioned above are subject to various terms and conditions, and the amount eligible for deductions may vary based on your income and other factors. Always consult a tax professional for personalized advice and guidance on deductions.
